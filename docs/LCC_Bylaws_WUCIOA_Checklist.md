## Article II — Members
**Badges:** 🔴 fix needed | ⚠️ flag | 🔵 note

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| 1. Is the verbiage clear? | Mostly. "Membership fee" is nonstandard — WUCIOA uses "assessment." |
| 2. Relevant RCWs / in line? | RCW 64.90.005 (definitions — "unit owner"); RCW 64.90.300 (powers). Tying membership to fee payment may conflict with RCW 64.90.300 (owners are members by operation of law). Annual fee-setting by board only may conflict with RCW 64.90.525 (budget ratification rights). |
| 3. Protects membership, board, or assets? | Protects assets (dues collection), but may inadvertently disenfranchise delinquent owners before a lawful due-process procedure. |
| 4. Repeatable / supports transition? | Partially — annual board-set fees are repeatable, but no cap, appeal, or ratification mechanism is stated. |
| 5. Easy or hard to change? | Membership composition should be harder to change than it currently is. |
| 6. Responsibility on an officer or agent? Backstop? | Board sets fees — no officer designated. No backstop if board fails to set fees in a year. |
| 7. Predictable? | Partially — "annual basis" is clear; timing of fee-setting is not. |
| 8. Fair in application? | At risk: tying membership/voting rights to fee payment without a cure process may violate RCW 64.90.530. |
| 9. Enables participation or oversight? | Partially — one lot/one vote is clear; no mention of member rights, meeting rights, or inspection rights. |
| 10. What if this fails? | Board fails to set fees → unclear whether members retain voting rights that year. |
| 11. What if abused? | Board could set assessment at a level that suppresses participation; no ratification safeguard. |
| 12. How would this be challenged? | Under RCW 64.90.525 (budget ratification), members already have a statutory override right regardless of bylaw silence. |

**Corrections required:**
- Membership tied to fee payment conflicts with RCW 64.90.300 — owners are unit owners/members by operation of law.
- No budget ratification process stated; RCW 64.90.525 already gives members this right but bylaws create a false impression of unchecked board authority.
- "Ch. 24.03 RCW" reference needs updating to Ch. 24.03A.

**Suggested revision:**
> "Members of the corporation shall consist of all persons and entities who own one or more lots within the community as described in the Articles of Amendment and Modified Protective Covenants 2008. Membership attaches to ownership and is not contingent on payment of assessments, except as provided by law with respect to voting rights in an enforcement action. Annual assessments shall be established by the Board of Directors pursuant to a budget process consistent with RCW 64.90.525, including member ratification rights."

Add cross-reference to Article III §3.8 (quorum by lot) for clarity on what "one lot/one vote" means when the same person owns multiple lots.

## Article X — Amendments
**Badges:** 🔴 fix needed | ⚠️ flag

| Question | Answer |

## Summary of Findings

### Items requiring correction before the draft advances (fix needed)

1. **Art. II** — Membership tied to fee payment conflicts with RCW 64.90.300. No budget ratification process stated.
2. **§3.3 / §4.3** — Mandatory 15-minute owner-comment period omitted. Effective 1/1/2026 under SB 5129 / RCW 64.90.415. Every board meeting held since that date without a comment period is a statutory violation.
3. **§3.5 / Art. VII** — Electronic notice not authorized. Mail-only notice conflicts with RCW 64.90.415.
4. **§3.7** — No secret ballot requirement for contested elections. "Any other method" language risks authorizing email-only voting.
5. **§4.1** — "Ch. 24.03 RCW" and "Ch. 64.38 RCW" are both repealed effective 1/1/2028. Must be updated globally. Sign regulation power overstates authority relative to RCW 64.90.510.
6. **§4.3** — No open-meeting requirement stated. No executive session procedure. Direct conflict with RCW 64.90.420 and SB 5129.
7. **§4.9** — Written consent provision may be used to circumvent open meetings. Ratification language internally inconsistent.
8. **Art. VI** — No reserve fund segregation, no investment limits, no reserve study requirement. Direct conflict with RCW 64.90.550 and SB 5129.
9. **Art. IX** — No member inspection right, no acknowledgment timeline, no production deadline. Mandatory under RCW 64.90.475.
10. **Art. X** — Board unilateral amendment authority is incompatible with WUCIOA. Undefined "adversely impacts" standard. No member petition right.

### Gaps requiring committee resolution before finalizing (flag)

- §3.8 — 50% quorum likely unworkable for 289 lots. Add adjourned meeting fallback.
- §3.4 — Omits member petition right to call a special meeting (already exists by statute).
- §4.1 — No term limits. Flag for committee decision.
- §4.2 — No officer designated to call a crisis-level special meeting.
- §4.7 — No member appeal process for ACC decisions. No posting requirement for committee minutes.
- §4.8 — No stated petition process to initiate a removal vote.
- Art. V — No conflict-of-interest disclosure or recusal procedure.
- Art. VIII — No indemnification review process; no exclusion for bad-faith conduct.

### Cross-references to resolve when companion articles are drafted (note)

- "Ch. 24.03 RCW" citation error appears throughout — correct globally at restatement.
- Article VIII indemnification should be paired with a companion board resolution defining the evaluation process.
- Article VI reserve fund expansion requires a companion reserve study policy and investment policy.
- §4.3 open-meeting additions require a companion executive session policy enumerating permitted grounds.
- Article VII notice update should be paired with a member-preference registry process managed by the Secretary.
