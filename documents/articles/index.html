Purpose 8 — Levy & Collect Assessments

To fix, establish, levy, and collect such charges and assessments as may be necessary, in the judgment of the Board of Directors, to carry out any or all of the purposes for which this corporation has been formed, and to file and foreclose a lien or liens upon any property or lot for which any assessment is not paid when due, together with interest and attorneys fees and costs in connection therewith.

Committee Notes

⚠️ Active internal conflict with Covenants Article VI.D. This purpose grants the Board unilateral authority to "fix, establish, levy, and collect" assessments as it sees fit, with no member vote required. Covenants Article VI.D, however, requires a 60% affirmative member vote to levy special assessments for capital improvements. These two documents pull directly against each other on who controls special assessments. The restatement must resolve this conflict explicitly — and WUCIOA's assessment framework (RCW 64.90.525) will govern regardless, so the resolution should align with that standard.

Purpose 10 — Engage in Any Lawful Activity

In furtherance and not in limitation of the above, to engage in any other lawful activity that may hereafter be authorized from time to time by the Board of Directors, and to exercise any or all powers of nonprofit corporations and of homeowners' associations pursuant to the laws of the State of Washington, including Ch. 24.03 RCW, the Washington Nonprofit Corporation Act, and Ch. 64.38 RCW, the Washington Homeowners' Associations Act, as amended.

Committee Notes

🟡 Governing law references will become stale. The Articles explicitly invoke Ch. 24.03 RCW (Nonprofit Corporation Act) and Ch. 64.38 RCW (HOA Act) as the governing framework. Both are being repealed or superseded by 2028 when WUCIOA (RCW 64.90) takes full effect. The statutory transition happens automatically — the Articles don't need to be amended just because of WUCIOA — but these statutory citations will become dead references and could create interpretive confusion during the window between now and a formal restatement. The restatement should update these citations to reflect current governing law.
