"In managing our parks, we've learned the most effective way to discourage negative behaviors from happening on our park lands is to have consistent and positive recreational use. So our fear is that if the access from your neighborhood was closed off, we would likely have less positive use and less eyes on the park property. The City also has a long-term goal of providing parks within walking distance (one-half mile) of all residents, and this pedestrian access would make the park walking distance for most (if not all) of LCC's residents."

"That being said, our preferred option is, and always has been, for the LCC to acquire the lot. We feel that this would enhance quality of life for your residents by giving them convenient access to 75 acres of forested open space. Research also shows that properties adjacent to parks like this also typically have higher home values. Most importantly though, we think your residents consistently utilizing our property is the most effective way to improve safety on the property and discourage nuisance behaviors."

"Please know that if the LCC chooses to purchase this lot or another lot or easement adjoining our Kaiser Woods Park property, we would be eager to work together in creating a safe access point into the park."

The covenants and bylaws permit a walking path.

"Based on my review of the documents listed above, I believe the Protective Covenants and Bylaws of Lakemoor permit the Club to install a walking path on the parcel. Furthermore, based on my analysis below, I believe it is permissible to use Lot 135 to provide pedestrian access to the Parcel from Lakemoor Drive, for the benefit of the residents of Lakemoor."

Article VIII(E): "No lot shall ever be used for or dedicated as a street or other public way to areas outside of Lakemoor Division 2 through 5." The project contemplated here is not a street or public way.

This is what we had!

Bylaws: The Bylaws grant broad powers to the Directors of Lakemoor. Improvement of a walking path on land owned by the Club is clearly within the scope of the Board's powers under Paragraph 4.1.1 of the Bylaws.

This shows that acquiring an easement or walking path is within scope.
